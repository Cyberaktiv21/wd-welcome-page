ePMR Technology (26TB-32TB)

Energy-assisted Perpendicular Magnetic Recording represents the current generation of HDD technology, delivering reliable storage in the 26TB to 32TB range. This technology uses refined magnetic recording techniques to achieve higher areal densities.

ePMR Technology (28TB-36TB)

Extended ePMR capabilities pushing toward 36TB through enhanced media optimization and improved write head technology. This represents the final evolution of conventional perpendicular magnetic recording.

HAMR Technology (36TB-44TB)

Heat-Assisted Magnetic Recording uses laser heating to temporarily reduce the coercivity of magnetic material, enabling writing to much smaller areas. This breakthrough technology enables capacities from 36TB to 44TB and represents a major leap forward in HDD density.

HDMR Technology (80TB-100TB)

Heat Dot Magnetic Recording builds upon HAMR technology with even more precise heating control, enabling massive capacities of 80TB to 100TB per drive. This technology represents the future of ultra-high-density storage.

Beyond 100TB

The future of HDD technology extends beyond 100TB per drive, with continued refinements to HDMR and potential new technologies on the horizon. This represents the long-term vision for magnetic storage.

Revolutionary HDD Architecture

Modern HDD architecture involves sophisticated coordination between mechanical precision, advanced materials science, and intelligent firmware. New architectures optimize every component from the read/write heads to the motor systems to achieve unprecedented storage densities while maintaining reliability.

OptiNAND + UltraSMR

OptiNAND integrates NAND flash memory directly on the HDD, enabling advanced caching and metadata management. Combined with UltraSMR (Ultra Shingled Magnetic Recording), which overlaps data tracks like roof shingles, this technology maximizes capacity while maintaining performance for sequential workloads.

Ongoing Innovation

HDD innovation continues across multiple fronts: helium-filled drives reduce turbulence and power consumption, advanced servo systems enable tighter track spacing, and sophisticated error correction algorithms maintain data integrity at increasingly higher densities. Power efficiency improvements make modern high-capacity drives more sustainable than ever.
